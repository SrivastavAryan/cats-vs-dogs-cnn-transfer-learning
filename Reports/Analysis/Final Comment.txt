The custom 4-block CNN outperformed the frozen ResNet50 transfer-learning model
across accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. The custom CNN achieved 83.83%
accuracy and 0.8360 F1-score, while ResNet50 achieved 61.92% accuracy and 0.6124
F1-score, giving the custom CNN an accuracy advantage of about 21.9 percentage points.

The custom CNN learned task-specific cat/dog visual features directly from the
training data and converged better, reducing loss by 59.4%. The ResNet50 baseline
reduced loss by only 11.3%, likely because the frozen ImageNet feature extractor
had limited adaptation capacity and the preprocessing/input-resolution setup was
not fully optimized for ResNet50.

Global Average Pooling was used in both models and helped reduce parameter count
and overfitting risk compared with Flatten-based classifier heads. The custom CNN
used only 390,850 parameters, while ResNet50 had 23.59M total parameters but only
4,098 trainable parameters because the base model was frozen.

Training times were comparable at 275.4s for the custom CNN and 282.9s for
ResNet50. Overall, the custom CNN performed better in this constrained setup,
while the transfer-learning baseline highlighted the importance of preprocessing,
input resolution, and fine-tuning strategy.
